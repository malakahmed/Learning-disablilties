Causes of Learning disabilities Effects of learning disabilities
Heredity: Often, learning disabilities run in the family, so it’s not uncommon to find that people with learning disabilities have parents or other relatives with similar difficulties. Learning disabilities causes troubles such as keeping words and letters in reading in order, spelling, and writing; keeping numbers in order in math; keeping track of a class schedule; completing long-range assignments, such as a term paper; and following directions.
Problems during pregnancy and birth such as injuries during or before birth;also because of low birth weight, lack of oxygen, drug and alcohol use during pregnancy, and premature or prolonged labor. As a result they will have a memory deficit that can cause problems with taking notes in class; remembering homework assignments, spelling or vocabulary; recalling information studied for tests; remembering basic math facts, phone numbers, addresses, locker combinations, people’s names, appointments.
Incidents after birth such as head injuries, nutritional deprivation, and exposure to toxic substances (i.e. lead) can contribute to learning disabilities. It can impact relationships with family, friends and in the workplace.
